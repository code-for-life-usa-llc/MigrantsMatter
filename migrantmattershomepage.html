Migrants and refugees face a multitude of struggles, including navigating complex legal and bureaucratic processes, enduring financial hardship, and facing discrimination and social isolation in their new environments. They may also experience mental health challenges, limited access to education and work, and difficulties in accessing healthcare. Migrant struggles encompass a wide range of challenges related to pre-migration, the journey itself, and post-migration experiences. These struggles can significantly impact mental health and overall well-being. Pre-migration factors can include poverty, lack of livelihood opportunities, violence, and persecution. During the journey, migrants may face dangerous conditions, exploitation, and limited access to basic services. Post-migration, they encounter difficulties such as social isolation, discrimination, language barriers, and legal uncertainties. Migrants are at increased risk of mental health issues like depression, anxiety, and PTSD due to trauma, displacement, and social isolation Migrants may experience prejudice and unfair treatment in the workplace and other areas of life Thankfully, MPP, also known as "Remain in Mexico", is a U.S. policy where certain non-Mexican migrants arriving at the U.S. southern border are returned to Mexico to await their U.S. immigration court proceedings. This program has been subject to legal challenges and has seen periods of implementation and termination under different administrations. MPP aims to address the situation at the border by managing the flow of asylum seekers and other migrants, while also prioritizing the safety and security of the U.S. and partner nations.
